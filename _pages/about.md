I am a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at the University of Florida, where I work in the Precision Space Systems Lab (PSSL). My current research is connected to the LISA (Laser Interferometer Space Antenna) mission, with a current focus on noise mitigation in the charge management device (CMD) testing instrumentation.

My research background is rooted in astrophysics, where I worked on pulsar timing and developed a simulator for ultra-wideband pulsar observations using Python and command-line tools. This work incorporated specialized packages such as psrsigsim and pulseportraiture to model and analyze high-precision timing data.

I began my academic career in a very different space, in Florida State University as a jazz trumpet performance major before transitioning into physics and astronomy. I completed my undergraduate degree in Physics with a specialization in Astronomy, along with minors in Mathematics and Music, at the University of Central Florida. Following this, I joined the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves (NANOGrav) and International Pulsar Timing Array (IPTA) Collaborations and spent two years as a research assistant under Dr. Joris Verbiest, where I focused on the aforementioned ultra-wideband pulsar timing simulations.

My broader goal is to develop as an interdisciplinary research scientist by building a diverse technical toolkit across computational, theoretical, and experimental domains. I am particularly interested in work that sits where the border between theoretical and experimental merge, where solving complex problems requires both analytical rigor and creative thinking. My technical experience is currently centered in scientific computing with Python, Linux/bash environments, and your classic microsoft softwares like excel. I am actively expanding into material based skills, including CAD modeling, cable fabrication, and soldering.

Beyond research, I am passionate about science communication and outreach. I have presented physics concepts at museums and public events, developing interactive demonstrations and visually engaging presentations that translate complex scientific ideas into engaging, accessible experiences for a wide range of audiences. and have participated in scientific conferences around the world through posters and talks.

Outside of academia, I maintain a strong connection to music and performance. I have trained and competed with multiple salsa dance teams and am currently a member of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers drumline. These experiences continue to shape how I approach discipline, collaboration, and creativity both in and out of the lab.
