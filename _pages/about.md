Hi, I’m Amanda!

I am a PhD student in Aerospace Engineering in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at the [University of Florida](https://mae.ufl.edu/), working in the Precision Space Systems Lab ([PSSL](https://pssl.mae.ufl.edu/)) under the guidance of [Dr. John W. Conklin](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=C-oDA-AAAAAJ&hl=en). 

I began my academic career in a very different space, studying trumpet jazz performance major at [Florida State University](https://music.fsu.edu/programs/jazz/) before transitioning into physics and astronomy. I completed my undergraduate degree in Physics with a specialization in Astronomy, along with minors in Mathematics and Music, at the [University of Central Florida](https://sciences.ucf.edu/physics/). Following this, I joined the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves ([NANOGrav](https://nanograv.org/)) and International Pulsar Timing Array ([IPTA](https://ipta4gw.org/)) Collaborations and spent two years as a research assistant under [Dr. Joris Verbiest](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=DKNMaqQAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao), where I developed a simulator for ultra-wideband pulsar timing observations using Python and command-line tools.

My current research is connected to the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna ([LISA](https://lisa.nasa.gov/)) mission, with a current focus on noise mitigation in the charge management device (CMD) testing instrumentation. My broader goal is to develop as an interdisciplinary research scientist by building a diverse technical toolkit across computational, theoretical, and experimental domains. I am particularly interested in work that sits where the border between theoretical and experimental merge, where solving complex problems requires both analytical rigor and creative thinking. 

Beyond research, I am passionate about science communication and outreach. I have presented at museums and public events, developing interactive demonstrations and visually engaging presentations that translate complex scientific ideas into exciting, accessible experiences for a wide range of audiences.

Outside of academia, I maintain a strong connection to music and performance. I have trained and competed with multiple salsa dance teams and am currently a member of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers drumline. As a child I heard the saying, "You can do anything you set your mind to", but no one said I had to choose one thing! Now satiating my curiosity comes in many forms. It shows up as axe throwing one week, gardening or baking the next, and often reading, writing, gaming, or traveling somewhere in between! Anything that keeps life from ever feeling routine.
